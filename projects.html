Projects & Research

This collection brings together GIS-based ecological research, interactive web projects, and visual communication work centered around wildlife, conservation, and environmental systems. The projects range from spatial analysis and StoryMaps to custom-built websites and interactive media designed to explore and communicate ecological relationships across landscapes.

GIS & Conservation Projects

These projects explore the intersection of wildlife ecology, spatial analysis, and real-world conservation challenges. Each project uses GIS-based methods to examine how human infrastructure and natural systems overlap, and where gaps in wildlife care and connectivity may exist.

Transportation + Wildlife Risk

Wildlife Collisions & Rehab Access — Texas

GIS-based analysis integrating roadkill occurrences, population density, transportation networks, and rehabilitation facility locations to identify gaps in wildlife response infrastructure.

Conservation Access Mapping

Wildlife Rehabilitation Access — Michigan

Spatial analysis of wildlife rehabilitation facility distribution across Michigan, examining relationships to transportation corridors and population density to identify potential access gaps.
