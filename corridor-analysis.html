Wildlife Corridor Connectivity Analysis

This project explores habitat connectivity through spatial resistance modeling to identify potential wildlife movement corridors across fragmented landscapes.

Project Goals

Evaluate landscape permeability and identify potential ecological corridors supporting wildlife movement.

Methods

Spatial analysis included resistance surface development, raster processing, and corridor modeling using GIS workflows.

Tools Used

ArcGIS Pro • Spatial Analyst • Raster Analysis

Outcomes

The analysis identified high-connectivity pathways between habitat patches, highlighting areas of potential conservation importance.
