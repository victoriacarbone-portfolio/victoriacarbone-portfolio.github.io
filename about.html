EcoGIS Studio

EcoGIS Studio explores ecological systems through spatial analysis, field observation, and geospatial mapping. The focus is on understanding how wildlife, landscapes, and environmental systems interact across space and time, and how these relationships can be visualized and interpreted through GIS. This work sits at the intersection of ecology and geospatial science, with an emphasis on using mapping tools to better understand real-world conservation challenges.

Research Approach

My work combines GIS analysis with ecological observation to interpret landscape patterns, habitat relationships, and environmental change. Rather than treating spatial data as purely technical output, I focus on using GIS as a way to tell ecological stories — connecting what is observed in the field with what appears in spatial data layers. This includes looking at how infrastructure, habitat fragmentation, and human activity influence wildlife movement, access to care, and environmental outcomes.

About the Researcher

I am an environmental and wildlife-focused GIS student building skills in spatial analysis, cartography, and conservation applications. My academic and field background includes wildlife rehabilitation, veterinary support, zoo education, and ecological research. These experiences shape how I approach GIS work — with an emphasis on real-world biological systems rather than purely technical mapping exercises. I am especially interested in using geospatial tools to support conservation decision-making, wildlife–human interaction studies, and accessibility analysis for wildlife rehabilitation systems. As I continue developing my technical skills, I am focused on strengthening my ability to translate ecological understanding into spatial insight through GIS-based analysis and visualization.
